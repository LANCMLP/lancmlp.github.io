Facts About Housing as an SDOH:

Poor housing conditions are linked to a multitude of poor health outcomes:

-Aggravation of asthma, allergies, bug bites, lead poisoning​

-Fires, electrocution, other physical injury​

-Tradeoffs between necessities (“heat or eat”), malnutrition, poor growth, arthritis pain, etc.​

-Behavioral issues, more common contraction of infectious disease, toxic stress​

Facts About Food Insecurity as an SDOH:

Children who live in households that are food insecure, even at the lowest levels, are likely to be sick more often, recover from illness more slowly, and be hospitalized more frequently.​

Lack of adequate healthy food can result in:​

-Reduced cognitive functioning​
-Impaired ability to concentrate and perform well in school
-Higher levels of behavioral and emotional problems from preschool through adolescence, and
-Developmental delays

Childhood malnutrition linked to adult diseases, including diabetes, hyperlipidemia and cardiovascular disease​

A recent study found that 30% of hospital “super-utilizers” were food insecure and another 25% marginally food secure by USDA definition, much higher than national average ​

Facts About Underinsurance as an SDOH:

Uninsured children are at greater risk for developmental delays that adversely affect educational achievement

People without insurance are more likely to:

-Have worse health outcomes, lower quality of life, and higher mortality rates
-Be diagnosed at later stages of their disease, when it is more difficult and expensive to treat their health conditions.
-Rely on emergency departments for routine care.
-Experience substantial economic and psychological stress due to health risks and financial uncertainty caused by the lack of health insurance
-People without insurance are less likely to receive preventive care, dental care, chronic disease management, and mental health care.

They are less likely to return for follow-up on visits, adhere to plans of care.
